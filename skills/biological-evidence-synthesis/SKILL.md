# Biological Evidence Synthesis

Synthesize what the evidence supports, not merely what the collected papers say.
Keep study results, validity judgments, and cross-study conclusions distinct.

## Intake

Require:

- A defined synthesis question and intended decision context.
- Included-study set with screening provenance and study-report links.
- Structured extraction data with outcome definitions and source locators.
- Protocol, planned subgroup logic, and any amendments.
- Relevant domain standards for validity and risk-of-bias assessment.
- Known dependence among reports, cohorts, sites, or experiments.

Stop and reconcile missing denominators, incompatible units, duplicate samples,
or unclear experimental units before quantitative work.

## Publication And Claim Verification

Before a paper contributes to a conclusion:

1. Verify its stable identity and distinguish preprint, accepted manuscript, and
   version of record.
2. Check current retraction, withdrawal, correction, and expression-of-concern
   status from authoritative publisher or index records.
3. Bind the extracted claim to a source passage, table, figure, or reported result.
4. Separate metadata verification from claim support: a real DOI does not prove
   that the paper supports the proposed statement.
5. Record unresolved status or inaccessible support as uncertainty, not acceptance.

Give eligible studies equal baseline treatment regardless of language, author
nationality, institution, country, or study location. Change confidence only from
documented study-level evidence, applicability, and bias assessment.

## Map The Evidence

Group studies by biological question, design, model system, population, exposure
or intervention, comparator, outcome construct, measurement method, and time point.

Create an evidence table containing:

- Study identity and design.
- Biological context and experimental unit.
- Groups, sample sizes, attrition, and analysis population.
- Outcome definition, method, scale, and timing.
- Effect estimate or directional finding with uncertainty.
- Validity limitations and relevant conflicts of interest.
- Applicability to the target question.

Do not count multiple reports or outcomes from one study as independent evidence.

## Assess Study Validity

Choose a design-appropriate, current risk-of-bias framework. Judge domains from
reported facts and source passages, not from study conclusions.

Consider:

- Allocation, randomization, concealment, and baseline imbalance.
- Blinding of participants, operators, outcome assessors, and analysts.
- Deviations from intended intervention or exposure classification.
- Missing outcomes, exclusions, attrition, and informative censoring.
- Measurement validity, assay reliability, and selective outcome reporting.
- Confounding, model specification, multiplicity, and analysis flexibility.
- Pseudoreplication, batch effects, contamination, and unit-of-analysis errors.

Record domain judgment, support, uncertainty, and reviewer resolution. Do not turn
a checklist count into an overall validity score unless the framework requires it.

## Examine Heterogeneity

Assess clinical or biological, methodological, and statistical heterogeneity.
Compare species or populations, context, dose or exposure, intervention delivery,
assay platform, outcome definitions, timing, design quality, and effect measures.

- Prespecify credible effect modifiers and subgroup contrasts.
- Distinguish explainable structure from unexplained inconsistency.
- Treat small subgroup counts and post hoc patterns as exploratory.
- Consider direction, magnitude, uncertainty, and prediction intervals where valid.
- Do not use a nonsignificant heterogeneity test as proof of homogeneity.

## Quantitative Synthesis Gate

Meta-analysis is allowed only when studies address a sufficiently coherent
estimand and effect measures can be validly harmonized.

Before pooling, document independence, unit compatibility, model choice, weighting,
between-study variance assumptions, small-study behavior, and handling of multiple
outcomes or arms. Obtain statistical review for complex dependence structures.

Do not pool merely because numeric results exist. Do not combine incompatible
designs, outcomes, time points, or biological systems into a misleading average.
When pooling is unjustified, use structured tables and a transparent narrative.

## Resolve Conflicting Evidence

For disagreements among studies:

1. Verify extraction, direction, denominators, and study-report identity.
2. Compare validity and risk-of-bias domains.
3. Examine population, model, context, measurement, timing, and implementation.
4. Check precision, selective reporting, multiplicity, and chance.
5. State which explanations are supported, plausible, or unresolved.

Do not resolve conflict by vote counting, prestige, publication date, or selecting
the result that matches the preferred mechanism.

Do not resolve conflict through a country, language, institution, or author
penalty. Formal source actions may exclude a specific version only when a
versioned record cites an authoritative retraction, withdrawal, regulatory or
institutional finding, or another reviewable basis and defines its scope and date.

Exclude a retracted or withdrawn version from positive support. Treat an
expression of concern, unresolved major correction, or documented ongoing formal
investigation as an enhanced-verification flag and do not rely on it alone for a
material conclusion. Informal allegations, social-media posts, news commentary,
and reputation claims do not trigger automatic exclusion.

## Rate Certainty

Use an explicit framework appropriate to the evidence type. Address risk of bias,
inconsistency, indirectness, imprecision, and reporting bias, plus justified
upgrading factors where the framework supports them.

Rate certainty per outcome or claim, not once for the whole literature. State the
target context, reasons for every rating change, and residual uncertainty.

## Artifacts

Deliver an evidence map, study-validity table, heterogeneity assessment, pooling
decision with rationale, synthesis results, conflicting-evidence analysis,
outcome-level certainty table, limitations, and prioritized evidence gaps.

## Non-Goals And Safety

- Do not invent missing results, convert absence of evidence into evidence of no effect,
  or use statistical significance as the sole measure of evidence.
- Do not make clinical recommendations beyond the review's evidence and mandate.
- Do not expose controlled or personal study data in synthesis artifacts.
- Do not conceal protocol deviations or post hoc analyses.
- Do not hardcode author or institution exclusions in this skill body.

## Completion Criteria

Complete only when study dependence is resolved, validity and risk of bias inform
interpretation, heterogeneity is characterized, pooling is explicitly justified or
rejected, conflicting evidence is explained without vote counting, and certainty
is reported for each main conclusion.
