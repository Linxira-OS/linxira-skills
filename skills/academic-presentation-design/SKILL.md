# Academic Presentation Design

Build the deck for spoken understanding, not for document completeness. A slide
is successful when the audience can identify the point within a few seconds and
the speaker can add the detail orally.

## Inputs

Require:

- audience type: lab, thesis committee, conference, mixed domain, or executive
- time limit and expected Q&A length
- delivery mode: live, recorded, remote, or poster-style walkthrough
- required figures, tables, and citations
- whether the deck is persuasive, educational, update-oriented, or defensive

## Default Flow

For most research talks:

1. Question and significance
2. Study design or dataset
3. Method or workflow at the level needed for trust
4. 2-5 main results, one point per slide or slide cluster
5. Interpretation, limitation, and comparison to prior work
6. Takeaway and next step
7. Backup slides for likely questions

Do not turn methods, references, and every control plot into main-line slides if
they interrupt the story. Move them to backup unless the audience must judge the
method itself.

## Slide Rules

- One visual point per slide whenever possible.
- Prefer a short takeaway title over a topic label.
- Keep body text sparse; use the speaker track for detail.
- Enlarge labels and simplify legends from manuscript figures.
- Put the citation or source marker on slides that present external findings,
  adapted figures, or methods.
- Ask for user-provided real figures or screenshots before substituting an
  illustration.
- Use animation only when it clarifies sequence or comparison.

## Figure Adaptation For Slides

- Crop or split dense manuscript figures into readable slide panels.
- Remove nonessential labels that the speaker can explain verbally.
- Add callouts only when they point to the exact evidence the audience should
  notice.
- Keep color meaning stable across the deck.

## Citation And Backup Practice

- Use a short inline citation on the slide and keep the full reference in notes,
  appendix, or an ending references slide when needed.
- Label AI-generated visuals explicitly and keep them out of evidence slides.
- Put reviewer-style sensitivity analyses, cohort details, and additional plots
  in backup slides, not between main claims.

## Completion Criteria

- The main line fits the speaking time without rushing.
- Each slide has an obvious spoken purpose.
- Citations are present wherever external evidence or adapted media appears.
- Backup slides cover the predictable questions without bloating the main deck.
