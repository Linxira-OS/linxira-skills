# Research Manuscript Integrity

Treat a manuscript as a traceable argument, not polished text alone. Every
claim must connect to supplied evidence, a verified source, a stated inference,
or an explicit proposal. Load this skill before presenting research prose as
complete, submission-ready, or supported by citations.

## Boundaries

This skill governs argument integrity and evidence disclosure. It does not
select a journal template, generate a DOCX or PPTX, search a restricted
database, alter a reference manager, or decide authorship. Use a matching
document, browser, data, or account contract before taking those actions.

## Build An Evidence Ledger

Before drafting substantive prose, classify each material claim as one of:

| Label | Meaning | What to preserve |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Source-derived | Directly supported by a cited paper, dataset, protocol, or record. | Stable citation or artifact identifier and the supported scope. |
| User-confirmed | Supplied or confirmed by the user. | Exact wording, provenance, and any stated limitation. |
| Analysis-derived | Produced by a described analysis. | Input version, method, parameters, statistical assumptions, and result artifact. |
| Interpretation | Reasoned explanation of evidence. | Basis, alternative explanation, and uncertainty. |
| Proposal | Recommended future work, hypothesis, or plan. | It must not be phrased as an observed result. |

Do not convert an absent value, missing figure, assumed parameter, or plausible
background fact into a result. Ask for the source or mark the gap.

## Citation Discipline

- Do not invent authors, titles, venue, year, pages, DOI, PMID, accession, or
  publication status.
- Cite a source only for the claim it actually supports. A topical citation is
  not evidence for a specific quantitative, causal, or methodological claim.
- Keep primary sources, reviews, preprints, and unverified references distinct.
- Verify retraction, withdrawal, correction, and expression-of-concern status
  before relying on a paper for a substantive conclusion.
- Do not run broad public-opinion searches for authors. Use publisher notices,
  bibliographic status records, and identified formal institutional or
  regulatory findings; treat informal allegations only as unverified leads.
- When metadata or full text is unavailable, use a visible placeholder such as
  `citation to verify` rather than a fabricated formatted reference.
- Preserve the user's existing citation keys and reference format unless a
  verified style requirement calls for a change.
- Do not assign credibility from publication language, author nationality,
  institution, country, or study location. Apply the same claim-support and
  study-quality checks to every source.

## Draft With Claim-Evidence Structure

For each section, identify its purpose before prose:

| Section | Required argument |
| --- | --- |
| Abstract | Question, approach, evidence, constrained result, and conclusion. |
| Introduction | Established context, unresolved gap, research question, and contribution. |
| Methods | Reproducible inputs, protocol, software, parameters, assumptions, and exclusions. |
| Results | Observation first, supporting artifact second, interpretation separated. |
| Discussion | Meaning, limits, competing explanations, and relation to prior work. |
| Conclusion | Answer supported by the work, limits, and bounded next steps. |
| Rebuttal | Reviewer concern, change or reasoned response, and exact manuscript location. |

Write precise statements with units, conditions, comparison baselines, sample or
dataset identity, and uncertainty where available. Replace generic praise or
certainty language with observable facts. Preserve formulas, variable names,
and measured values unless the user confirms a correction.

## Review Before Delivery

Check each of the following:

1. Every result traces to a supplied or reproducible artifact.
2. Every citation has verified metadata or an explicit verification marker.
3. Observations, interpretations, and proposals are not conflated.
4. Methods include enough detail to understand scope, inputs, parameters, and
   limitations.
5. Figures and tables have source, unit, condition, and uncertainty context.
6. Limitations and negative or inconclusive outcomes remain visible.
7. The requested format, language, journal, or institutional constraints are
   either met or explicitly listed as unresolved.

## Completion Record

State what was drafted or revised, which evidence was used, which citations or
claims still need verification, and which formatting or source-access limits
remain. Never report a manuscript as submission-ready when essential evidence,
citations, permissions, or required review are unresolved.
