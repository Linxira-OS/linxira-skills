# Wet-Lab Experiment Planning

This skill is planning-only. It organizes authorized work around current SOPs; it
does not create protocols or replace biosafety, ethics, welfare, or clinical oversight.

## Mandatory Stop Gate

Before planning proceeds, require:

- A named institution, facility, supervisor, and authorized personnel.
- Current authoritative SOP identifiers, owners, versions, and effective dates.
- Applicable approvals, permits, registrations, and protocol numbers.
- Confirmed training, competency, access, and required supervision.
- Hazard assessment, containment assignment, emergency response, and waste route.
- Data classification and approved storage for samples and records.

Stop if an SOP is absent, expired, conflicting, or unavailable; approvals do not
cover the work; personnel are untrained; containment or waste handling is unclear;
or the request optimizes a pathogen, toxin, regulated agent, or dangerous procedure.

## Intake

Collect only planning-level information:

- Scientific objective, approved study identifier, and success criteria.
- Experiment owner, operators, facility, room class, and scheduled window.
- Sample classes, source restrictions, storage constraints, and chain of custody.
- Expected controls, experimental units, batches, and analysis handoff.
- Known hazards, incompatibilities, exposure routes, and affected personnel.
- Dependencies, hold points, stopping rules, and escalation contacts.

## Authoritative Procedure Check

Use controlled documents from the responsible institution, regulator, manufacturer,
or other recognized authority. For each governing document record:

- Exact title, identifier, revision, owner, approval status, and effective date.
- Scope, facility, personnel, materials, and equipment covered.
- Required approvals, training, controls, records, and emergency references.
- Any conflict with another instruction and the authority that resolved it.

Never reconstruct a missing SOP from memory or generic protocols. Route any network
or browser access through `research-web`.

## Approval And Training Matrix

Link every planned activity to its approval, facility authorization, role, training,
competency requirement, and supervisor.

Verify, as applicable:

- Institutional biosafety and chemical safety review.
- Human-subject, consent, privacy, clinical, or animal-welfare authorization.
- Organism-use, environmental, import, transport, radiation, controlled-substance,
  regulated-agent, or specialized-equipment approval.
- Facility access, occupational health, fit testing, and emergency training.

## Hazards And Waste Plan

Reference the institutional risk assessment. Map material and activity categories
to hazard, containment, controls, PPE, emergency response, decontamination, waste,
labeling, storage, transport, and responsible contact.

Flag incompatible waste, unknown mixtures, sharps, contaminated disposables, and
licensed-pickup materials. Do not improvise deactivation, disposal, or emergencies.

## Reagent And Sample Provenance

For every sample or reagent category, record:

- Identifier, source, supplier or custodian, and receipt date.
- Lot, batch, passage, generation, or collection metadata where applicable.
- Certificate, authentication, quality status, expiration, and storage record.
- Consent, use restriction, permit, transfer agreement, and data classification.
- Chain of custody, aliquot identity, inventory location, and disposition.

Quarantine unclear, expired, mislabeled, or unauthorized material under local policy;
do not relabel or dispose of it without authorization.

## Planning Artifacts

Produce a planning packet that references, but does not extend, the approved SOP:

- SOP and approval register with current versions.
- Training and authorization matrix.
- Sample and reagent provenance manifest.
- Control, batch, plate, and equipment allocation plan.
- Hazard, waste, emergency, and data-handling matrix.
- Pre-start, intermediate, release, and closeout checkpoints.

## Checkpoints And Stopping Rules

Define checkpoints for approvals, material release, equipment, controls, sample
identity, data capture, and waste closeout.

At each checkpoint record owner, evidence, pass criteria, hold status, and escalation.
Stop on deviation, unexpected hazard, containment failure, identity mismatch, failed
critical control, equipment fault, exposure, spill, custody loss, or approval breach.
Follow the authoritative incident procedure and notify the institutional contact.

## Deviation And Closeout Records

Record deviations with date, operator, affected items, containment, notification,
authorization, impact, and disposition. Never backfill approval or edit silently.

Closeout covers sample disposition, inventory, equipment, waste evidence, deviations,
raw-data location, access controls, and supervisor sign-off.

## Non-Goals And Safety

- Do not provide execution parameters or substitute for hands-on training.
- Do not optimize growth, yield, delivery, scale, virulence, toxicity, evasion,
  dissemination, deactivation resistance, or other operational performance for
  pathogens, toxins, regulated agents, or dangerous procedures.
- Do not advise bypassing controls, approvals, containment, monitoring, or records.
- Do not expose controlled sample, participant, genomic, or facility information.
- Do not claim that a planning packet authorizes work.

## Completion Criteria

Complete only when current authoritative SOPs and approvals are verified; personnel,
facility, hazards, waste, training, provenance, and data controls are documented;
checkpoints and stopping rules have owners; deviation and closeout records are
defined; and no requested content crosses into unsafe operational optimization.
